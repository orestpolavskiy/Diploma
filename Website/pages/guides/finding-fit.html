Fit Guide

Finding Your Fit

Finding the right fit is the single thing that makes every garment look better. It doesn't matter how good the fabric is — if the shoulders are off or the waist sits wrong, the piece won't work. Here's how to use our size charts.

Reading The Measurements

Size charts give you numbers. You need to match those to your body measurements. For most of our pieces, the three key measurements are chest (or bust), waist, and seat.

Measure yourself with a tape measure, not a ruler. Hold the tape snug but not tight — you want the actual measurement, not an aspirational one. For tops and jackets, measure the widest part of your chest. For trousers, measure your natural waist (roughly an inch above the hip bone) and the widest part of your seat.

Then compare your measurements to the garment measurements in the product listing, not just the suggested size label. Garment measurements tell you the actual dimensions of the finished piece. They're more useful than a number on a label, because different manufacturers size differently.

How Our Cuts Run

We design in two fits: relaxed and tailored.

Relaxed cuts add 4–6 cm of ease on the chest and have a straighter leg or torso. They're designed to be comfortable with a layer underneath. If you're between sizes in a relaxed cut, go down — the extra room is already built in.

Tailored cuts run closer to the body with about 2–3 cm of ease. They're designed to be worn alone or over a very fine base layer. If your chest and waist measurements are close to the garment measurements, that's correct — it's not too tight, that's how the cut is meant to sit.

Coats and outer layers always have more ease than knitwear or shirts to allow for layering.

When To Size Up Or Down

Size up if you're between sizes and the piece is a structured outer layer (coat, jacket, blazer). You want room for a mid-layer underneath. Also size up if you plan to machine-wash rather than hand-wash — most of our natural fibre pieces have minimal shrinkage, but it's worth accounting for it.

Size down if you're between sizes and the piece is a fine knit or a base layer. You want it to sit close to the body without pulling.

  • If your chest and seat measurements fall in different sizes, order the size that fits your larger measurement and have the other adjusted by a tailor.
  • For trousers, the seat measurement is usually the harder one to adjust — prioritise that and have the waist taken in if needed.
  • Our size charts are updated with each collection. If you're unsure between two sizes, contact us before ordering.
