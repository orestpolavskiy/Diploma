What We Are Wearing This Season

Every few seasons, a particular way of dressing rises to the top. Right now it's something we'd call quiet tailoring — clothes that look sharp without making any noise.

The Quiet Tailoring Moment

The loud end of fashion has been very loud for a while. Oversized logos, bright colours, deliberate irony. There's a turn happening now — back towards pieces that are well made and relatively unremarkable at first glance. A blazer that fits properly. Trousers that break cleanly at the shoe. A shirt tucked in.

This isn't nostalgia for formality. It's not about dressing up. It's about clothes that are clearly intentional without announcing themselves. You notice the quality before you notice the shape, and the shape before you notice anything else.

We've been making pieces in this direction for a while. It feels good to see the wider conversation catch up.

Why Grey Returns

Grey is doing a lot of work this season. Not the cool, slightly blue-grey of a few years ago — a warmer, more complex grey, somewhere between charcoal and stone. It pairs with almost everything in a tonal wardrobe: navy, cream, camel, black.

The appeal is partly practical. Grey sits between the warmth of beige tones and the sharpness of black, which makes it the most versatile colour in a limited palette. It also photographs well and ages well — one of those colours that tends to look better after a few wears than it does brand new.

We'd recommend it as a base colour rather than an accent. It's not trying to be interesting. It just works.

The Relaxed Trouser

Slim trousers had a long run. They're not gone, but the silhouette we keep coming back to is looser: a mid-rise trouser with a straight leg that tapers slightly above the ankle. Not wide enough to be a statement. Not narrow enough to pull.

This cut works because it's easy to wear. It doesn't need a specific shoe to look right. It works with a tuck-in and an untuck. It's comfortable for a full day without looking like loungewear.

We've been making versions of this trouser in wool-blend and in a heavier cotton canvas. Both sit well with the quieter tailored pieces we mentioned above. If you're only going to buy one new pair of trousers this season, this is the cut to go for.
